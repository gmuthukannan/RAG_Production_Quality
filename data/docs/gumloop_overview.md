# Gumloop company brief for a RAG application

## Overview
Gumloop is an AI-powered, no-code automation platform that lets users build workflows and agents by connecting modular components on a visual canvas.[1][2] The company positions the product as a way for non-technical users and business teams to automate repetitive or complex work without needing to code.[1][3]

## Founding+
Gumloop was officially founded in April 2023 by Max Brodeur-Urbas and Rahul Behal, two McGill University classmates.[4] The company originally operated under the name AgentHub before rebranding to Gumloop in 2024 to sound more accessible to non-developers.[4][5]

## Funding history
Gumloop announced a $3.1 million seed round in July 2024 led by First Round Capital, with participation from Y Combinator and a group of angel investors including Max Mullen, Arash Ferdowsi, Andrew Ofstad, Immad Akhund, Kulveer Taggar, Liquid2, WndrCo, Theory Forge Ventures, Richard Aberman, Ludlow Ventures, and others.[3]

In January 2025, Gumloop raised a $17 million USD Series A round led by Nexus Venture Partners, with participation from First Round Capital and Y Combinator.[5] BetaKit reported that this brought the company’s total funding to $20 million USD at that time.[5]

By March 2026, Gumloop reported a $50 million Series B led by Benchmark on its homepage, and external coverage said the round also included participation from prior investors such as First Round, Greylock, Nexus Venture Partners, Y Combinator, Box Group, and others.[1][4][6] Because investor lists differ slightly across sources, the safest phrasing is that Benchmark led the Series B and previous backers also participated.[1][6][4]

## Team
At the time of Gumloop’s July 2024 seed announcement, the company described itself as having two technical co-founders plus an intern.[3] In January 2025, BetaKit reported that the team consisted of co-founders Max Brodeur-Urbas and Rahul Behal plus one employee, with plans to hire more in San Francisco.[5]

Known leadership details include Max Brodeur-Urbas serving as co-founder and CEO.[7] Public source material in the collected set consistently identifies Rahul Behal as the other co-founder, but a fully current public team page was not retrieved in this research batch, so the broader team should be treated as evolving.[4][5]

## What Gumloop can do
Gumloop allows users to create drag-and-drop AI workflows, often called flows, by chaining nodes that handle individual steps in a process.[3] These workflows can connect data sources and business tools such as Google Sheets, Slack, YouTube, websites, email, and other apps.[5]

Examples published by the company and external sources include invoice extraction from PDFs, programmatic SEO content generation, news analysis and reporting, application filtering, customer research, sales outreach, and document question-answering workflows.[3][8] Recent third-party descriptions also highlight autonomous agents, reusable skills, direct chat with agents, and integrations with Slack, Microsoft Teams, and email.[9]

For a RAG use case specifically, Gumloop has visible community interest and walkthrough content around document-based Q&A, knowledge bases, and retrieval workflows using uploaded or connected documents.[10][8][11] This suggests it can support practical RAG-style applications, especially for teams that want a low-code or no-code orchestration layer rather than building the entire stack from scratch.[10][8]

## Who could benefit
Gumloop appears most useful for non-technical operators, business teams, and small companies that want AI automation without hiring engineers for every workflow.[3][5] The company explicitly says it aims to remove the coding barrier so the people who understand business problems can automate them directly.[3]

Based on the customer examples cited by Gumloop, likely beneficiaries include operations teams, finance and accounting teams, marketing and growth teams, recruiting teams, agencies, research-heavy organizations, and startups building internal tools or lightweight AI products.[3] Enterprises may also benefit because current pricing and packaging references mention RBAC, SCIM/SAML, audit logs, custom retention, and VPC options in enterprise plans.[12]

## RAG-specific relevance
For a RAG application, Gumloop is best viewed as an orchestration and automation layer rather than a pure vector database vendor.[10][8] It is a fit when the goal is to quickly assemble ingestion, document parsing, retrieval logic, agent behavior, and delivery into tools like Slack or email using visual workflows.[8][9][5]

Potential RAG scenarios include internal knowledge assistants, policy and SOP search, research-paper Q&A, contract or invoice lookup, and support knowledge bots.[8][3] It may be especially attractive for teams that value rapid prototyping, business-user ownership, and broad app integrations over custom-coded infrastructure.[3][5]
